# How to Use Launch Command Center

## Who does what

**Launch PMM or Program Manager (the owner)**
You set up the launch, own the full tracker, and are the single source of truth. You update tasks, escalate blockers, and send the exec summary to leadership.

**Function Leads** (Product, Sales Enablement, Legal, etc.)
You update the status and tasks within your own workstream. You can add notes, flag questions, and tag other functions if something impacts them.

**Leadership**
You receive the weekly exec summary via email or Slack. The PMM generates it with one click from the Exec Summary tab.

---

## Setting up a new launch

1. Open the app — you'll land on the setup wizard
2. **Step 1:** Enter the launch name, a brief description, and your target launch date
3. **Step 2:** Enter the revenue inputs
   - *Target revenue month* — the month leadership expects revenue from this launch
   - *Average sales cycle (weeks)* — your best estimate from first deal to close
   - *Pipeline target* — the dollar amount expected in motion at launch
4. **Step 3:** Check every function involved in this launch
5. Click **Open Command Center**

The revenue risk engine starts calculating immediately. If your launch date plus your sales cycle pushes past the target revenue month, a red banner fires on the dashboard.

---

## Managing workstreams day to day

Go to the **Workstreams** tab and expand any function.

- Set the **Function Owner** name at the top of each workstream
- Click **+ Add Task** to add milestones and deliverables
- Each task has: name, owner, due date, and status
- Status options: Not Started → On Track → At Risk → Blocked → Complete
- When you mark something **At Risk** or **Blocked**, a blocker type and description field appear — fill these in so the issue is visible to everyone

Tasks marked overdue (past due date, not complete) are automatically flagged.

---

## Comments, notes, and tagging other functions

Every task has a **Comments & Notes** section at the bottom of the edit modal.

To add a note:
1. Click the ✏ edit icon on any task
2. Scroll to the Comments section
3. Enter your name, your note or question, and optionally tag one or more functions using the **@Tag** chips
4. Click **Post Note**

Tagged comments surface in the **Activity** tab so the relevant function leads can see anything flagged for them. This is how you flag a cross-functional dependency or question without losing it in Slack.

---

## Sending the weekly exec summary

Go to the **Exec Summary** tab. The summary is generated automatically from the current state of all workstreams. It includes:

- Overall launch status
- Revenue risk status
- Which functions are on track, at risk, or blocked
- All active blockers with owner and type
- Any decisions needed from leadership

Click **Copy to Clipboard** and paste directly into your leadership email or Slack message.

---

## Multi-user setup note

This tool currently runs in your browser and saves data locally. That means:

- **One person owns the setup and master data** (the PMM)
- **Others can view the live app** at the same hosted URL
- **For team updates**: function leads report status to the PMM, who updates the tracker — or the PMM shares their screen during launch syncs

A shared real-time version with individual logins is on the roadmap for a future release.

---

## Tips

- **Set it up before your launch kickoff** and walk the team through it in the first sync
- **Update status at least weekly** — the exec summary is only as good as the data in it
- **Use the Blockers tab in leadership reviews** — it shows every blocked or at-risk item by type, so the right people can resolve them in the room
- **Revenue risk is a forcing function** — if the banner fires, that's your signal to either accelerate the launch or reset expectations on revenue timing
